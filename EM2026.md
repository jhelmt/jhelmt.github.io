# Explanation
The Examiners Mile, also known as the Departmental mile, was first ran in 1973 following a coffeetime conversation between two members 
of staff in the Mathematics department. They argued whether it would be possible for a reasonably fit individual to be within 50% of a 
world record time and, so story has it, they failed to break six minutes for a mile. Thus the annual race was born to be run on the day 
before finals results are published. The record is a sizzling 4:31.41 held by Peter Grime. (Text by James Blowey)

Past websites of results can be found [here](https://maths.dur.ac.uk/Local/mile.html) and [here](https://www.maths.dur.ac.uk/Local/moremiles.html)
